CHAPTER 1: SYNOPSIS
The Synopsis of a project serves as a comprehensive summary of its purpose, scope,
implementation strategy, technical components, testing mechanisms, and impact. For the
CarWorthML system, a machine learning-based car price prediction web application, the
synopsis consolidates the technical rationale, problem motivation, software architecture,

shaped its development.
1.1 Statement about the Problem
In the Indian automobile market, and in similar emerging economies, the used car segment
constitutes a substantial portion of total car sales. With more than 4 million used vehicles
exchanged annually, determining a fair market price for a pre-owned car has become
increasingly difficult due to the absence of standard valuation tools. Unlike new cars, which
have fixed pricing from manufacturers, the value of used cars is influenced by a multitude of
variables: brand, model, fuel type, kilometers driven, ownership history, maintenance record,
location, and even visual condition.
The problem arises from a lack of transparent and reliable systems that consider these variables
holistically. Currently, both buyers and sellers rely on incomplete data or heuristic
approximations, often comparing listings on various websites like OLX, Quikr, or CarTrade.
However, such listings often do not reflect actual sale prices, are outdated, or lack completeness
in terms of features (e.g., missing kilometers or ownership history).
This causes several systemic issues:
1 Sellers may underprice their vehicle, resulting in financial losses.
2 Buyers may overpay or purchase undervalued vehicles without knowledge of the fair price.
3 Dealers may exploit the pricing ambiguity, reducing trust in the resale ecosystem.
The absence of a data-driven, ML-powered solution in this space is both a technical and
commercial gap—one that CarWorthML is designed to fill.

CHAPTER 2: PROBLEM FORMULATION
Problem formulation is the initial phase in a project’s lifecycle where the nature, scope,
background, and need for a solution are identified and clearly articulated. For CarWorthML, a
machine learning-based used car price prediction system, this chapter lays the conceptual and
foundational groundwork by addressing the what, why, and how of the project.
2.1 Introduction about the Project
In the age of digital transformation, data-driven technologies are playing an increasingly
significant role in simplifying real-life decision-making processes. One such domain that stands
to benefit enormously is the automobile resale market, which often lacks transparency and data-
backed decision tools. The CarWorthML project is a machine learning-powered web application
that aims to bridge this gap by providing accurate, real-time predictions for the resale value of
used cars.
CarWorthML uses historical car listing data collected from online classified platforms,
processes it using data cleaning techniques, and trains a predictive model using regression-
based algorithms. The result is a lightweight, responsive, and scalable web application built
using Python and Streamlit, which allows users to enter a few details about their vehicle and
receive an estimated resale price instantly. By leveraging the power of machine learning, the
project aims to reduce the uncertainty in used car pricing and provide equitable value
estimations for both buyers and sellers.
2.2 Introduction about the Problem
In India and across many emerging markets, the used car industry is flourishing due to growing
consumer demand, affordability concerns, and the increasing lifespan of vehicles. However,
this surge in demand is not matched by a consistent system for evaluating the resale value of
used cars. Pricing decisions are typically based on subjective estimates, heuristic guesses, or
comparison across various listings, which can be misleading due to regional disparities,
inconsistencies in car conditions, and lack of structured information.
The core of the problem lies in the absence of a standardized, accessible, and data-backed
platform that can determine the fair market price of a used vehicle. Buyers risk overpaying for
cars that are not worth the quoted price, while sellers might undervalue their cars due to poor
market awareness. Dealers, on the other hand, often exploit this information asymmetry to their
advantage.
This lack of transparency not only creates distrust but also reduces the overall efficiency and
fairness of the vehicle resale process. CarWorthML seeks to address this problem by providing
a user-friendly tool that uses machine learning to deliver realistic car price estimations based
on past sales data and current trends.

2.3 Present State of the Art
Currently, several platforms such as CarDekho, CarTrade, and OLX Autos offer car valuation
tools as part of their vehicle listings. These tools, however, come with certain limitations:
1 Black-box Models: Many commercial platforms do not reveal how they arrive at a given
price, making it difficult for users to trust or understand the valuation process.
2 Limited Feature Inclusion: Often, only a few basic inputs are considered, such as brand,
model, and year, while important factors like kilometers driven, fuel type, and market
trends are overlooked or generalized.
3 Non-personalized Estimates: These platforms tend to provide price ranges instead of
specific estimates, which might not align well with the actual condition or usage of the car
in question.
From a technical perspective, most existing solutions are built on rule-based heuristics or basic
averaging models, which lack the adaptability and learning capability of machine learning
algorithms.
In contrast, CarWorthML is a transparent, open-source project that allows users to see how input
features directly impact the outcome. It employs supervised learning and is trained on a cleaned
dataset of real-world used car listings, providing a more accurate and personalized estimate.
2.4 Need for Computerization
Manual methods of estimating used car prices are not only time-consuming but also unreliable.
These methods depend on:
1 Browsing through hundreds of listings to find similar car models.
2 Asking for suggestions from friends, dealers, or mechanics.
3 Relying on subjective negotiation and guesswork.
These practices introduce bias, waste time, and result in either underpricing or overpricing.
Moreover, in the digital era, where most services are accessible at the click of a button, car
valuation should be no different.
Computerizing the process offers numerous benefits:
1 Speed: Instant price predictions reduce the time spent on market research.
2 Accuracy: The model uses mathematical relationships between features and pricing trends
to deliver consistent results.
3 Accessibility: The web interface makes it available to anyone with internet access—buyers,
sellers, and dealers alike.
4 Transparency: Users gain insights into the logic behind the prediction, building trust in the
valuation process.
Therefore, computerization using machine learning models like the one in CarWorthML is not
just beneficial but essential in modernizing the used car market.

2.5 Proposed Software / Project
The proposed software, CarWorthML, is a responsive web application that predicts the resale
price of used cars based on structured user input. The application is designed to:
1 Accept five input parameters from the user: car brand (company), model name, year of
manufacture, fuel type, and kilometers driven.
2 Feed this data into a pre-trained machine learning model based on Linear Regression,
which was trained on thousands of real car listings.
3 Return a predicted resale value in INR that closely reflects current market trends.
4 Provide additional insights such as average price by company and fuel type for context.
Key Components of the Software:
1. User Interface: Developed using Streamlit, which offers a clean layout, interactive
widgets, and mobile responsiveness.
2. Data Handling Layer: Uses Pandas and NumPy for data manipulation, cleaning, and
transformation.
3. Machine Learning Model: A Scikit-learn regression pipeline trained on a cleaned
dataset.
4. Deployment Setup: Localhost-based app using Streamlit with optional deployment on
Streamlit Cloud or similar platforms.
By integrating these components, CarWorthML functions as a complete and deployable
machine learning system tailored to the Indian automobile resale ecosystem.

CHAPTER 4: SYSTEM DESIGN
1. Design Methodology
System design is a critical stage in the software development life cycle (SDLC) that determines
how the overall architecture of the system is structured. For the CarWorthML project, a modular
and layered design methodology was adopted to ensure scalability, maintainability, and
separation of concerns.
The system is structured into three primary layers:
1 Presentation Layer (Frontend): Developed using the Streamlit library, this layer is
responsible for user interaction. It collects input from users through UI elements like
dropdowns, sliders, and buttons, and displays the prediction results and data visualizations
in a visually appealing and responsive manner.
2 Application Layer (Backend Logic): This layer processes user input, transforms it
into a format suitable for the machine learning model, loads the trained model, and
produces predictions. It also handles preprocessing steps, error handling, and integration
between components.
3 Data Layer: This includes static data files such as Cleaned_Car_data.csv, which simulates
the role of a database by storing historical car listings used for both training the model and
visualizing price trends. Additionally, this layer includes the trained machine learning
model stored as a serialized .pkl file.
This design ensures a decoupled structure where the user interface, business logic, and data
remain independent, allowing future enhancements to any one layer without impacting the
others significantly.

6.3 Limitations of the System
Although CarWorthML is functionally complete and performs effectively within its designed
scope, several limitations have been identified that restrict its use in more complex or
commercial environments.
1. Static Dataset
The current version uses a cleaned CSV file as its primary data source. While this approach
simplifies development, it limits the model's ability to adapt to changing market conditions or
incorporate new trends automatically.
2. Limited Features
Only a few features are considered for prediction (company, model, year, fuel type, and
kilometers driven). Critical real-world factors like car condition, service history, accident
records, location, transmission type, number of owners, and insurance status are not yet
incorporated, potentially affecting prediction accuracy.
3. Basic Model Complexity
The model used is a Linear Regression pipeline. While effective for linear relationships, it may
not capture complex interactions between variables or account for outliers and irregular pricing
trends. More robust models (e.g., Random Forest, Gradient Boosting) may yield better
performance in dynamic markets.
4. No Persistent User Data
The application currently operates statelessly. There is no feature for saving user history,
exporting reports, or managing sessions. This limits its usefulness for repeat users or
commercial businesses requiring audit trails.
5. Local Deployment Only
While the system runs perfectly in local environments, deployment to web servers for public
access requires manual configuration. No CI/CD pipeline or user authentication is
implemented, restricting it from being used as a production-grade SaaS platform in its current
form.
